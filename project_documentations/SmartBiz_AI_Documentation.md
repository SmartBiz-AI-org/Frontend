# SmartBiz AI Documentation

**Enyata × Interswitch Buildathon 2026**\
**Role:** Project Manager

## Executive Summary

In a 5-day national buildathon, I led a cross-functional team to design
and deliver SmartBiz AI---an AI-powered commerce assistant that enables
Nigerian SMEs to convert customer conversations into completed payments
in real time.

The core challenge was not just technical delivery, but orchestrating
multiple dependencies (AI, payments, messaging) under extreme time
pressure while ensuring a flawless live demo.

To succeed, I made three critical decisions early:

1.  Ruthlessly reduced scope to only demo-critical features

2.  Prioritized speed over perfection in technology choices

3.  Enforced structured execution with zero tolerance for instability

As a result, we delivered:

-   A fully functional end-to-end product (Vendor onboarding → chat →
    payment → invoice → notification)

-   0 critical bugs in final delivery

-   100% success rate across multiple test runs

-   A flawless live demo

This case demonstrates my ability to lead under pressure, make
high-stakes trade-offs, and deliver reliable outcomes in constrained
environments.

## Problem & Opportunity

Across Nigeria's 40M+ SMEs, customer acquisition increasingly happens
via messaging platforms like WhatsApp. However, most businesses lack the
infrastructure to convert conversations into transactions.

**Before SmartBiz AI**

-   Customer inquiries often go unanswered for hours

-   No structured flow from interest to payment

-   Payments handled manually or externally

-   High drop-off and lost revenue

**After SmartBiz AI**

-   Instant AI-powered responses

-   Guided purchase flow within chat

-   Embedded payment links

-   Automated invoice and confirmation

**Opportunity:**\
Bridge the gap between conversation and conversion, turning messaging
platforms into transactional sales channels.

## **Pre-building Phase 1: Foundation** 

At the outset of the project, I took ownership of transforming an
initial idea into a structured, executable product vision. I
conceptualized the buildathon direction, defined the problem space, and
translated it into a clear MVP strategy that aligned both with user
needs and delivery constraints.

To bring this vision to life, I led the recruitment of a
cross-functional team, carefully selecting members based on
complementary skill sets and their ability to execute within a
fast-paced environment. I then developed a comprehensive MVP brief,
outlining the product concept, core features, and expected outcomes,
ensuring the team had a shared understanding of what success looked
like.

To drive alignment from day one, I facilitated an onboarding session
where I communicated the product vision, clarified roles, and delegated
responsibilities. This created early momentum and established
accountability across the team.

However, this phase was not without challenges. Midway through team
formation, I identified a critical risk: some initial team members were
unable to commit due to the project's complexity and time demands.
Rather than allowing this to derail progress, I made the decision to
restructure the team early. I quickly replaced unavailable members with
more committed contributors, prioritizing reliability and execution
capacity over initial familiarity.

This decision, although difficult at the time, proved pivotal. It
ensured that the team entered the build phase with the right level of
commitment and capability, ultimately setting a strong foundation for
successful delivery.

Other things I did in this phase include:

## Phase 2: Integration --- Building Phase

This phase represented the most execution-intensive stage of the
project, with significant risk arising from dependencies across design,
frontend, backend, and third-party integrations. My primary focus was to
maintain momentum, ensure alignment, and proactively resolve blockers
before they could impact delivery timelines.

To achieve this, I drove execution across three core areas:

1\. Team Coordination & Delivery Management

-   Led daily standups via Google Meet to track progress, surface
    blockers, and reinforce accountability across the team

-   Implemented a structured check-in approach ("progress, pending,
    blockers") to ensure clarity and consistency

-   Proactively followed up with team members to resolve blockers
    quickly, minimizing delays across dependent tasks

-   Maintained delivery momentum by ensuring no critical task remained
    stalled beyond a short turnaround window

2\. UI/UX Alignment & Quality Control

-   Coordinated closely with the designer to obtain the Figma prototype
    of SmartBiz AI

-   Facilitated live review sessions via Google Meet to walk through the
    design, identify usability gaps, and document required improvements

-   Ensured design iterations were completed promptly by following
    through until all feedback was fully implemented

-   Maintained alignment between product vision and user experience to
    prevent rework during development

3\. Frontend & Backend Execution Oversight

-   Translated design into execution by handing off finalized Figma
    assets to the frontend developer with clear expectations and
    timelines

-   Conducted multiple live review sessions to validate implementation
    accuracy and ensure consistency with the approved design

-   Personally tested key user flows, validating that buttons,
    interactions, and features functioned as intended

-   Oversaw backend development and AI integration, ensuring all
    components worked cohesively

-   Identified and resolved integration issues with third-party systems,
    including Telegram, preventing potential breakdowns in the user
    journey

## Phase 3: Submission

In this phase, I collated all team documentation, tested the MVP hosted
on Vercel to ensure proper functionality, recorded the MVP demo, and
submitted the team's work via the submission link provided by the Enyata
team.

The submission included:

-   MVP link

-   Individual contributions of each team member

-   Pitch deck

-   Team code

At this stage, the primary risk shifted from system functionality to
demo reliability.

Key Decisions & Trade-offs

1.  Interface choice: Initially, I planned to use WhatsApp as the user
    interface, but this required Twilio integration, which was a paid
    feature. As an alternative, we used Telegram to remain within
    budget.

2.  Feature scope control: Multiple potential feature additions were
    deferred to protect delivery quality and maintain focus.

3.  Speed over production readiness: Supabase was selected as the
    database due to its ease of use, prioritizing rapid integration over
    full production-level optimization.
